  <section class="about-story">
    <p>I'm a senior aerospace engineer at Cornell, spending more time in the Swanson computer lab and the ELL workshop basement than I'd like to admit. Most of my work has been at Cornell's Design Build Fly, where I've had four years of experience as a member, subteam lead, and now full-lead, working through every part of the design cycle. From the time rules come out in August, to our competition in April, I am non-stop at work: ideating, redesigning, improvising. Everything always breaks the first time around. Then the second. Then, if you're unlucky, the third. But I couldn't imagine anything different. Never do I learn so much in such a short time, and never is it as rewarding.</p>

    <p class="about-story-full">Along the way I've gotten to touch a lot of different corners of the field. On the test side, I've built thrust rigs, run wind tunnel experiments, and wired up flight controller data loggers to make sense of what was actually happening in the air. On the design side, I've built genetic algorithm sizing tools, prototyped morphing camber wings, and contributed to nine competition aircraft worth of carbon fiber and balsa. On the simulation side, I've modeled propellers computationally, run full aircraft CFD in Fluent, and built an AVL-backed flight dynamics simulator from scratch. Each project pulled me somewhere I hadn't been before, and that's honestly been the whole point. I'm looking for a full-time role where that doesn't stop.</p>
